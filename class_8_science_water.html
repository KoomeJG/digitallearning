Water

Soft and hard water

  • Water, in its natural state, is pure and does not contain dissolved mineral salts.
  • Water without dissolved mineral salts is said to be soft
  • Water with dissolved mineral salts is said to be hard water
  • Water from oceans, lakes,seas, boreholes, rivers and dams is said to be hard water.
  • Rain water is soft water.
  • Soft water can form lather easily with soap.
  • Hard water does not lather easily with soap.

Disadvantages of hard water

  • It wastes fuel when boiling to soften it.
  • Wastes soap when used for washing.
  • Wastes time since it leads to delays while boiling or being used to wash clothes.
  • When transported while hot through pipes, it forms boiler's scale that can end up bocking the pipes.
  • Discolours clothes when used for washing.
  • Stains the container in which it is boiled.
  • Interferes with dying and tanning process.

Softening hard water

  • The process of removing minerals from hard water is called softening.
  • Hard water can be softened through boiling and use of chemicals.
  • Boiling hard water removes temporary hardness.
  • Permanent hardness can be removed by using chemicals
  • Use of chemicals for removing permanent hardness in water is commonly done in industries.
