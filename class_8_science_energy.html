Energy

Introduction

Energy is the ability to do work. Work is only considered done when force is applied over some distance

Types of energy

There are different types of energy.

Chemical Energy

  • This is also known as stored energy.
  • Chemical energy is found in food, dry cells, batteries, fuels such as firewood, petrol, kerosine and diesel.
  • The chemical energy in fuels is released when they burn.
  • The body is able to store the food we eat as chemical energy.

Heat Energy

  • Heat energy is mainly obtained from the sun.
  • Heat energy can also be obtained from electrical or any burning fuel. Rubbing two surfaces also produces heat.
  • Heat energy can be used for cooking, ironing clothes, warming ourselves and drying.

Light Energy

  • Light Energy mainly comes from the sun.
  • Other sources of light energy include candles, electric bulbs, gas lamps and gas lihters.

Magnetic Energy

  • This is the force of attraction from a magnet.
  • Magnetic energy can be used to lift heavy containers at the port

Electricity

  • This is the energy produced from chemical reactions taking place in things like batteries.
  • There are two types of electricity namely static and current electricity.
  • Static electricity comes from charged particles as a result of rubbing
  • Current electricity comes from car batteries, dry cells, bicycle dynamoes, hydroelectric generators, geothermal generators, wind driven turbines, solar panels, petrol and diseal driven generators.
  • Electric energy can be used in different ways such as lighting a house, running different electrical appliances, cooking and for running big machines in factories.

Sound Energy

  • This is a form of energy produced by vibrating objects.
  • The speaker in a radio vibrates to produce sound.

Transformation of energy

  • The change of energy from one form to another is called transformation.
  • Energy can neither be created nor destroyed. It can only be transformed from one form to another.
  • The existance of energy is only noticed during its transformation from one form to another

Transformation of energy in an electric circuit

  • An electric circuit is a closed path which allows an electric current to flow
  • During the flow of an electric circuit the following transformations of energy occur. Chemical energy-electrical energy - heat + light energy

Transformation of energy in food

  • The food we eat has stored energy (chemical energy)
  • The food is broken down in the body to produce heat energy that keeps the body warm.
  • Heat energy also helps in motion and doing work.
  • Energy in motion is known as kinetic energy

    • Chemical energy - Heat energy - Kinetic energy

Transformation of energy in burning fuels

  • Fuels have chemical energy
  • The chemical energy is transformed to heat during burning.
  • The heat energy can then be used for warming or cooking
