Plants

Adaptation in plants

The features a plant develops to enable it to survive in its natural environment are known as adaptations.

Adaptations of plants that grow in dry areas

  • The plants develop deep root system to enable them get water from under ground.
  • They have tiny leaves, or the leaves may be reduced to thorns, to reduce the surface area for loss of water.
  • Some shed their leaves to reduce water loss through transpiration -these are known as decidous plants.
  • Some are succulent -they store water in their stems.
  • Some fold their leaves to reduce the surface area for water loss.
  • Plants that grow and survive in dry areas are called Xerophytes. Examples of xerophytes are acacia,baobab,bombax,Casuarina,Sisal and euphorbia

Adaptation of plants that grow in wet areas

  • Some, like the mangrove, develop breathing roots.
  • Some have floating flowers to allow pollination.
  • The leaves have thin cuticles to increase loss of water
  • They have Shallow roots to reduce the amount of water absorbed.
  • They have flexible stems to ensure that they are not broken by water waves.
  • They have broad, large leaves to increase water loss.
  • They have air sacs on the leaves that enable them to float on water..
  • The leaves have many stomata on the the upper side of the leaves to increase water loss.
  • Plants that grow and can survive in wet places are calledhydrophytes
  • Examples of hydrophytes are Water lily,Water hyacinth,water lettuce,Mangrove,buttercup,rice,aquatic fern,,duck weed and sea weed

Signs of unhealthy crops

  • Stunted growth
  • Discolouration of leaves and stems
  • Curled leaves
  • Wilting
  • Spots and streaks on leaves
  • Distortations and malformations

Effects of crop diseases

  • Lower yields
  • Reduced quality of produce
  • Increase in cost of production
