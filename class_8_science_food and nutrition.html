Foods and Nutrition

Nutrition requirements for special groups

Special groups are people who require more of particular nutrients in their bodies than others. They include:

  • Pregnant women
  • Lactating mothers
  • HIV and AIDS patients
  • Infants

Nutriational requirements for pregnant women

  • Expectant women require a balanced diet.
  • Proteins are required for the growth of the developing baby.
  • Minerals, like calcium are required for building the bones of the developing baby.
  • Some minerals,such as iron, help in blood formation.
  • Vitamins are required for protection against diseases.

Nutritional requirements for lactating mothers

  • Lactating mothers require a balanced diet.
  • The diet should have extra fluids for milk production.
  • Iron is needed in blood formation to help the lactating mother recover the blood she lost while she was giving birth.
  • Minerals, such as calcium and phosphorus, are needed for the formation of strong bones and teeth

Nutritional requirements for infants

Infants are mainly fed on breast milk early in their lives.

The value of breast milk

  • It contains all the nutrients required by the baby.
  • It contains colostrum and other components that boost child's immunity hence reducing the chances of infection.
  • Helps the mother and the baby to bondS
  • Does not require preparation
  • It is easy to digest

Nutriational needs of people with HIV and AIDS

  • A balanced diet rich in proteins and vitamins
  • Proteins help in building worn out body tissues
  • Vitamins help in protecting the patients from opportunistic diseases like flu and cold.
  • Fried fatty foods should be avoided.
  • Feeding should include small portions of food at a time.

Food poisoning

  • Food poisoning is caused by chemicals and micro-organism.
  • Chemicals that can cuse food poisoning include detergents, acaricides, pesticides and insecticides.
  • Micro-organisms that can cause food poisoning are bacteria and fungi.

Symptoms of food poisoning

  • Vomiting
  • Fever
  • Body weakness
  • Diarrhoea
  • Stomach ache
  • Dizziness

Prevention of food poisoning

    Left over foods should be warmed properly before being consumed.
    Mouldy foods should not be consumed.
    Foods eaten raw such as fruits and vegetables should be washed before eating.
    Foods to be warmed before eating should be warmed thoroughly.
  • Any left-over foods should be stored properly so that they do not become stale.
  • Kitchen waste should be disposed of well or put in a bin that can be covered.
  • Chemicals used at home should be stored safely away from food.
