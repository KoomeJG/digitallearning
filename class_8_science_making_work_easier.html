Making Work Easier

Simple machines

  • A machine is a device on which force is applied to make work easier.
  • The simple machines we are going to study are Inclined planes and Single fixed pulleys.
  • Chancroid

Inclined planes

  • An inclined plane is a slope. It can be gentle or slopy..
  • An inclined plane makes work easier by increasing the effort distance.
  • Examples of inclined planes include:
    • Ladder
    • A ramp
    • Spiral stairs
    • Ladder
    • A road winding up a hill
    • Staircase
    • Escalators
    • Screws

Using an inclined plane

  • Place two ladders of similar height on the wall one forming a steep slope and the other forming a gentle slope.
  • Climb the two ladders one at a time with a friend holding it securely.
  • Which ladder is easier to climb?
  • Which ladder is more difficult to climb?.

Observation

  • It is easier to climb the ladder with a gentle slope.
  • It is more difficult to climb the ladder with a steep slope.

Conclusion

It is easier to climb the ladder with a gentle slope because it reduces the steepness up the wall. Staircases, escalators and spiral stairs make it easier to climb to a floor of a building by reducing the slope and increasing the effort distance.

Road winding up a hill

Vlimbing up a long the winding route is much easier as the road is not so steep. However, one has to travel a much longer distance to reach the top.

Screw

  • The distance between the threads on the screw determines how easy it is to use.
  • When the threads are far apart, it becomes difficult to drive the screw.

Single fixed pulley

  • A single fixed pulley can be used to lift heavy loads from lorries or into them.
  • It can be used in raising a flag.
  • Single fixed pulleys can be used by break-down vehicles to pull vehicles involved in accidents or mechanical problems.
  • Single fixed pulleys can also be used at construction sites to lift heavy loads and building materials.

Using a single fixed pulley

  • When the effort is pulled, the load moves in the opposite direction.
  • When the effort is pulled down 1 meter, the load moves up 1 metre.
  • The effort distance is equal to the distance moved by the load.
  • The pulley wheel changes the direction of the force applied.

Measuring the effort used in a single fixed pulley

  • The effort or force applied on a single fixed pulley can be measured using a spring balance.
  • The spring balance is fixed on the effort side.
  • How the spring balance stretches shows how much effort is used to lift the load.
  • The effort used is usually slightly more than the load.
