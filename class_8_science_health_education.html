Sexually transmitted infections

Sexully transmitted infections are infections caused by germs spread through sexual contact. Sexually transmitted infections include:

  • Syphillis
  • Gonorrhea
  • Chancroid

Syphilis

  • It is caused by bacteria and is spread through sexual intercourse.
  • It can also be spread by kissing an infected person and also from mother to child.
  • It starts with a sore that appears 2 weeks after infection.
  • Appears like open sores on open organs and also in the mouth
  • Rashes on the skin and patches on the face appear.
  • Spreads to the brain and heart
  • It can be spread to the child in the womb incase the mother is infected.
  • It can lead to madness because it affects the brain.
  • Leads to pain in the joints and may eventually lead to paralysis.

Gonorrhoea

  • It is caused by bacteria and spreads through sexual intercourse.
  • It affects the urethra leading to the production of pus and pain when passing urine. In females, it affects the vagina.
  • It causes pain in the lower abdomen.
  • It can lead to infertility, if not treated in good time.

Chancroid

  • It is caused by bacteria spread through sexual intercourse.
  • Sores appear on the thigh, hands and sex organs.
  • The rectum is also affected and can lead to breeding.

Prevention and control of STIs

STIs can be controlled in the following ways:

  • Avoiding sexual contact with infected persons.
  • Mother to child infection should be avoided.
  • Abstaining from sex if not married
  • Seeking treatment in case of infection
  • Avoiding drug abuse that can lead to poor decision making or judgement
  • Having discussions on the dangers of STIs

Control Measures for HIV and AIDS include:

  • Creating public awareness on HIV and AIDS.
  • Campaigning through various media.
  • Voluntary counseling and testing(VCT)
  • Educating the mass on the dangers of HIV and AIDS.

Ways of creating awareness on HIV and AIDS

  • Conducting awareness during meetings such as chief's barazas, security meetings and family gatherings.
  • Creating awareness in churches and crusades..
  • Educating people about HIV and AIDS in market places and during funerals and weddings.
  • Sexual contact with an infected person.

Ways of conducting campaigns through the media

  • Various campaign messages can be passed to the masses through radios, televisions, newspapers, brochures, magazines, video, programs and journals

Importance of voluntary counseling and testing centres

  • Counseling and testing centres help people to accept their status and avoid spreading the virus.
  • The counseling centre helps one to understand that being HIV posotive is not the end of the world
